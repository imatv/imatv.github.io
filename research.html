Rissman Memory Lab

Under the supervision of Dr. Jesse Rissman, I currently work as a research assistant in a cognitive neuroscience lab at UCLA. Listed below are the projects that I am currently a part of:
  • With graduate student, Nicco Reggente, we are using functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) and diffusion tensor imaging (DTI) data from the Human Connectome Project (HCP) to account for individual differences in fluid intelligence, memory strength, and reward. I write Matlab and shell scripts to perform complex analyses over many subjects and examine the correlations to their behavioral scores on various IQ tests and tasks.

  • As a part of the DARPA-sponsored ALIVE (Avatar Learning in Virtual Environments) project, working under graduate students, Joey Essoe and Nicco Reggente, we are investigating the cognitive and neural mechanisms of learning that occurs within virtual reality (VR) and their implications for future training and educational purposes. I am currently undergoing MRI safety orientation and human participant ethics training in order to assist in the process of running participants.
For more details about the research that goes on in the lab, visit the lab page!
